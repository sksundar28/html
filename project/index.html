In 2007, MS Dhoni captained a young and relatively inexperienced Indian team to victory in the inaugural ICC World Twenty20.

In 2010, MS Dhoni led the Chennai Super Kings (CSK) to their first-ever IPL title, defeating the Mumbai Indians in the final. This victory marked the beginning of a dominant era for CSK in the tournament, with Dhoni's leadership playing a crucial role.

In the 2011 Cricket World Cup final, MS Dhoni's captaincy and performance were pivotal in India's victory against Sri Lanka. Leading from the front, he promoted himself in the batting order and delivered a match-winning, unbeaten 91 runs, including the iconic six that sealed the win.

In 2013, MS Dhoni led the Indian cricket team to victory in the ICC Champions Trophy, defeating England in the final by 5 runs. This win was particularly significant as it made Dhoni the first and only captain to win all three major ICC white-ball trophies: the T20 World Cup (2007), the ODI World Cup (2011), and the Champions Trophy (2013).
